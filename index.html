Exploring Light, Life and Thought

Hi – I’m Hubert Huho, a Biomedical Engineering student at Johns Hopkins University. I’m fascinated by the way light can be controlled, how living organisms build nanostructures and how the right diagram can change the way we think about science.

About Me

I grew up hearing stories from my parents’ homelands of Cuba and Kenya and visiting communities where healthcare resources are limited. Those experiences convinced me that engineering isn’t a luxury – it’s a way to expand access to life‑saving care. They’re why I chose Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins.

At Hopkins I work on a BME Design Team developing a scraper for lung cryobiopsy probes and contribute to a computational pathology project using QuPath and machine learning to detect actinic keratosis in confocal images. Beyond coursework in biomedical data science, probability, and physics, I nurture curiosities in biomineralization, photonics and mental models through training programs and independent research. This site shares some of the projects and ideas that motivate me.
