Exploring Light, Life, and Thought

Hi – I’m Hubert Huho, a Biomedical Engineering student at Johns Hopkins University (’28). I’m fascinated by how light can be controlled, how living organisms build nanostructures, and how the right diagram can reshape how we think about science.

About Me

I grew up hearing stories from my parents’ homelands of Cuba and Kenya and visiting communities where healthcare resources are limited. Those experiences convinced me that engineering isn’t a luxury—it’s a way to expand access to life-saving care. That conviction led me to Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins.

I built this site for my Regeneron internship application and because I’ve always wanted a space like this. Beyond a résumé or cover letter, it shares the projects and ideas that have shaped who I am. From simulating twisted light inside tiny optical cavities to marveling at the mineral masterpieces forged by bacteria and algae—and dreaming up new visual languages for physics—this is a glimpse of my journey.
